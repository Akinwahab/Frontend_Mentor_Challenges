Akara (Nigerian Bean Cakes) Recipe

Akara is a popular West African breakfast dish made from peeled beans, blended with spices, and deep-fried into golden-brown fritters. Crispy on the outside and soft inside, they are enjoyed with pap (ogi), bread, or garri.

Preparation time

  • Total: Approximately 45 minutes
  • Preparation: 30 minutes
  • Cooking: 15 minutes

Ingredients

  • 2 cups black-eyed peas (peeled)
  • 1 medium onion (chopped)
  • 2 scotch bonnet peppers (or to taste)
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Seasoning cube (optional)
  • Oil (for deep frying)

Instructions

  1. Soak beans in water and peel off the skins by rubbing them between your hands.
  2. Blend peeled beans with onions and peppers until smooth (add very little water).
  3. Whisk the batter to incorporate air for a fluffy texture.
  4. Heat oil in a deep pan until hot.
  5. Scoop spoonfuls of batter into the oil and fry until golden brown, flipping halfway.
  6. Remove and drain on paper towels. Serve hot with pap, bread, or garri.

Nutrition
